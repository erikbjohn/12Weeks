## Agent 1: Strength & Power Programming (Phases 2–3 + 1RM Test)

**Recommended structure:**

- **Phase 2 (weeks 5–7) Strength block:** Wendler 5/3/1 with First Set Last (FSL) back-offs. Training Max = 90% of true 1RM. Wave: wk 5 5/5/5 @ 65/75/85%, wk 6 3/3/3 @ 70/80/90%, wk 7 5/3/1 @ 75/85/95%. FSL = 5×5 at the wk-1 first-set %.
- **Phase 3 (weeks 9–11) Peaking block:** Top-set + back-off intensity wave. Wk 9 1×3 @85% + 3×3 @75%; Wk 10 1×2 @90% + 2×3 @78%; Wk 11 1×1 @92% + 2×2 @80%. RPE cap at 8 on top set, 7 on back-offs.
- **Week 12 1RM test:** Mon Squat / Wed Bench + OHP 3RM / Fri Deadlift. Three working singles per lift: opener (~92% projected, RPE 7), second (~97%, RPE 8.5), third (goal weight ~100–103%, RPE 9.5–10). Convert to 3RM if HRV/sleep flags trigger.

**Cut adaptations:**

- Hold intensity, cut volume. Volume drops 20–30% vs. maintenance; top-set % does NOT drop.
- Extend rest 30–50% (3 min becomes 4–5 min) due to slower phosphocreatine resynthesis in glycogen-depleted state.
- Cap AMRAPs at +2 reps over the prescribed minimum (no true failure in deficit).
- Reduce assistance/accessory volume 25–40%.

**Compound progression rules** (top-set evaluation):

| Performance | Action |
|---|---|
| RPE ≤ 7, hit prescribed reps cleanly | +10 lb lower / +5 lb upper, increment TM |
| RPE 8, hit prescribed reps | +5 lb lower / +2.5 lb upper OR hold |
| Missed by 1–2 reps | Hold weight; if 2× in a row → drop TM 5% |
| Missed badly (≥3 short or technical breakdown) | Drop TM 10%, mini-deload |

**Auto-regulation triggers** (any 2 = unplanned mid-block deload):

1. Bar speed visibly slower at top % across 2 consecutive sessions
2. Resting HR up >10 bpm for 3+ days
3. Sleep <6 hr or fragmented for 4+ nights
4. 2 missed top sets in a single week
5. Mood/cognitive aversion to lifting (relevant given mental-health anchor)
6. Joint pain ≥4/10, especially shoulder/neck

**Shoulder/neck — strength-block specifics:**

- OHP: bar over mid-foot at lockout, no layback. ROM cap at chin if deeper hurts. Landmine press as substitute. Mandatory pre-press warm-up: 2 min band pull-aparts + face pulls.
- Bench: tucked elbows ~45°, paused, no narrower than shoulder-width.
- Bent-Over Row: controlled tempo (no Pendlay yank). Chest-supported alternative on reactive weeks.
- Weighted Pull-Up: full hang, neutral grip preferred over pronated, no kipping, no failure.
- **Excluded outright:** behind-neck press, upright row, behind-neck pulldown, heavy shrugs.
- **Daily 5–10 min:** banded pull-aparts, prone Y-T-W, foam-roll thoracic spine.

**Open questions:**

1. Current best estimate of true 1RMs on Squat/Bench/Deadlift/OHP? TM-setting depends on this.
2. Deficit magnitude (kcal/day)? Volume cut math depends on it.
3. Trail-run cost on next-day readiness — need 2–3 cycles to dial in.
4. Shoulder/neck — generalized tightness or specific impingement direction?
5. Strength priorities for week 12 — all four lifts PR, or 2 priority + 2 maintain?

## Agent 2: Hypertrophy + Cut-Phase Programming (Phase 1 + accessories all phases)

**Volume prescription (sets per muscle per week, cut-adjusted):**

| Muscle | Cut Phase 1 Target |
|---|---|
| Chest | 10–14 |
| Lats | 12–16 |
| Mid-back | 10–14 |
| Rear delts | 10–14 |
| Front delts | 4–8 direct (heavy indirect from press) |
| Lateral delts | 12–18 |
| Quads | 10–14 |
| Hamstrings | 8–12 |
| Glutes | 8–12 |
| Biceps | 10–14 |
| Triceps | 10–14 |
| Calves | 8–14 (run gives ~6) |
| Core | 8–12 direct |

**Cut adjustment factor:** ~15–20% reduction in recoverable volume per ~500 kcal/day deficit (Aragon/Schoenfeld 2020, Helms 2014). Phase 1 starts at MAV; weeks 5–7 collapse to MEV; weeks 9–12 hold MEV with intensity preserved.

**Rep range distribution:**

- **6–10 reps (heavy hypertrophy, RIR 1–3):** Squat, deadlift variants, RDL, BB row, bench, weighted pull-up, leg press top sets.
- **10–15 reps (moderate, RIR 1–2):** DB press variants, machine chest press, lat pulldown, seated row, Bulgarian split squat, leg curl/ext, hip thrust, EZ-bar curl, close-grip bench, dips.
- **15–25 reps (metabolic, RIR 0–1):** Lateral raises, rear delt flyes, face pulls, cable curls, triceps pushdowns, calf raises, ab work, banded lateral walks.

**Phase 2 mixing strength + pump same lift (e.g., Lat Pulldown 5×5 + 3×12 same day):**

- Defensible IF heavy precedes pump AND same primary mover.
- Rule: cap muscle's weekly set total at MAV. Lats getting 5×5 + 3×12 Mon plus 4×6 row + 3×10 row Thu = ~15 sets = high end of cut MAV.
- Two heavy compounds same muscle same day = NO. Antagonist superset = OPTIMAL.

**12-week volume curve:**

| Week | Phase | Volume | Intensity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | Hypertrophy | MAV (high) | RIR 1–3 |
| 4 | Deload | 50% volume, 70% load | RIR 4–5 |
| 5–7 | Strength + pump | MEV–MAV (mid) | RIR 0–2 top, 1–2 pump |
| 8 | Deload | 50% volume, 70% load | RIR 4–5 |
| 9–11 | Cut climax | MEV (floor) | RIR 0–2 heavy, 0–1 iso |
| 12 | Peak / refeed transition | MEV –20% | Maintain intensity |

**Where to cut volume first** (descending priority): direct work for muscles trained heavily indirectly (front delts), high-rep metabolic finishers when soreness lingers >48 hr, calves (run covers some). NEVER cut: primary compounds, lateral delts (cheap recovery), back volume.

**Exercise tier system (cut-optimized):**

| Muscle | Primary (heavy compound) | Secondary (machine/DB) | Accessory (cable/iso) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chest | DB bench (neutral if shoulder), Smith bench | Machine chest press | Cable fly, pec deck |
| Lats | Weighted pull-up, BB row | Lat pulldown | Straight-arm pulldown |
| Mid-back | T-bar row, BB row | Seated cable row, chest-supported row | Cable face pull |
| Rear delts | Reverse pec deck | Cable rear delt fly | Band pull-aparts |
| Front delts | Landmine press | Machine OHP | DB front raise (minimal) |
| Lateral delts | — | Cable lateral raise | DB lateral raise |
| Quads | Leg press, squat (Smith if neck) | Hack squat, Bulgarian split | Leg extension |
| Hamstrings | RDL | Leg curl machine | Single-leg curl, Nordic eccentrics |
| Glutes | Hip thrust | Cable kickback, machine hip thrust | Banded glute work |
| Biceps | Weighted chin-up, BB curl | DB curl (incline) | Cable curl, hammer curl |
| Triceps | Close-grip bench, dips | Cable pushdown | Overhead cable extension |

**Deload spec (weeks 4 & 8):** 50% volume, ~70% load, RIR 4–5, 5 days, full rest weekend, NO trail run that week. Exit criteria: HR within 5 bpm baseline, sleep ≥7/10 self-report 3 consecutive nights, no soreness >24 hr Mon after Fri session.

**Mental health framing:**

- Session length cap 45–60 min (Schoenfeld 2019).
- Low day-to-day exercise variability within a phase.
- "Minimum effective day" coded fallback: 3 working sets across 3 lifts, RPE 7, 25 min. NOT skipping. The floor.
- Behavioral anchor: TIME, not intensity.

**Saturday integration:**

- **Lift Saturdays:** full-body, low volume, upper-body emphasis. Pull-up 4×6, DB bench neutral 3×10, single-leg light 2×12, lateral 4×15, face pull 4×15, ab wheel 3×8. ~50 min.
- **Trail-run Saturdays:** Friday cuts lower-body indirect work. Sat = run. Sun = rest non-negotiable. Mon after = volume cut 30% on lower (hams take the descent hit).

**Open questions:**

1. Actual deficit magnitude week 1 vs. week 12 (kcal/day curve)?
2. Trail run consistent every-other-Sat or weather/schedule dependent?
3. Sleep data — <7 hr collapses cut MRV by ~25% (Schoenfeld 2018, Dattilo 2011).
4. Bodyweight measurement protocol — single morning weight or 7-day rolling? Single will trigger false plateaus.
5. Target end-of-cut bodyweight — physiologically realistic in 12 weeks? If >1% BW/week sustained, lean mass loss is expected even with optimal training (Garthe 2011).

## Agent 3: Adaptation to Erik (constraints + integration)

**Equipment availability flags (treat full inventory list as aspirational):**

- **Likely present:** adjustable dumbbells (~50–75 lb), barbell + bumper plates (300–400 lb max loadable), adjustable bench, pull-up bar, kettlebells (mixed gaps), bands, TRX, ab wheel, med ball.
- **At-risk:**
  - Leg curl/ext machines → DB RDL, single-leg DB RDL, TRX hamstring slider, Nordic curl progression
  - Power rack with safeties → assume NO spotter pins. Cap back squat RPE 8. Bias to front squat, goblet, Smith, leg press, split-squat.
  - Lat pulldown / cable stack → band-anchored pulldown, weighted pull-up, neutral-grip, TRX inverted row.
  - Seated row machine → chest-supported DB row, single-arm DB row, TRX row.
  - Chest press / Smith → flat/incline DB press, floor press.
  - Calibrated plates → assume 2.5/5/10/25/45. No microloading. **5 lb upper / 10 lb lower** is the practical jump floor.
  - DB ceiling — if 50 lb cap, heavy DB press/row outgrows by week 4–6. Plan barbell or Smith subs ahead.
- **Rule:** every prescribed lift needs a written substitute that uses only barbell, dumbbells, bench, pull-up bar, bands, TRX. Otherwise flag for Erik to confirm equipment.

**Shoulder/neck protocol (chronic, not acute):**

- **Excluded outright:** behind-neck press, upright row above chest, wide-grip BB bench at competition width, heavy high-bar BB squat if cervical compression, snatch-grip work, dynamic Olympic catches, heavy pronated shrugs.
- **Modified:**
  - OHP: neutral-grip DB, landmine, half-kneeling landmine. If BB OHP, cap ROM at forehead-clearance (no behind-the-ears). Push press over strict when heavy.
  - Pull-ups: bias neutral and supinated.
  - Bench: moderate grip, tucked elbows ~45°, pause variant > touch-and-go.
  - Lateral raise: cap at shoulder height, slight forward lean.
- **Mandatory pre-press/pull warm-up (5–7 min, non-optional):** band pull-aparts 2×20, scap push-ups 2×10, wall slides 1×10, neck CARs slow 3–5 each direction, banded shoulder pass-throughs 1×10. **Skipping = same as skipping a working set.**

**Trail-run Saturday integration:**

- El Cajon Mountain ~3,400 ft / ~11 miles round-trip / 2–3 hr. Treats as full lower-body equivalent fatigue.
- **Trail-run Saturdays:** NO lift. Run replaces lift entirely.
- **Friday before run-Sat:** Convert to upper-body only, moderate volume, no PR attempts. Skip heavy hinge/squat. Cap session 45 min.
- **Sunday after:** keep as rest. Optional 20–30 min mobility/band stretching only. Track Mon squat RPE post-trail vs. non-trail; if RPE consistently +1.5 same load, push Mon to Tue for trail-run weeks.
- **Lift-Saturday weeks (off-week):** arms/accessory/conditioning only. No squats, deadlifts, heavy press. 30–40 min.

**Cut-mode coaching constraints:**

- User priority is target weight, no rate caps. The program **does not** call "slow down on the cut" — it calls "**adjust the lift, not the diet**." If Mon squat top set drops 10% over 2 weeks at same RPE, reduce lift load. Cut continues.
- Volume gives way before intensity. Hierarchy when fatigue hits: (1) cut accessory volume −25%, (2) drop one exercise per session, (3) reduce frequency 6→5, (4) only THEN reduce intensity on main lifts.
- **Refeed days: ONE structured per week**, prescribed not optional, placed day-before trail-Sat OR day-before Mon squat (whichever is heavier-intensity that week). +50% carbs over baseline; protein/fat held.

**Mental-health programming choices:**

- **Consistency > variety.** Same compound lifts week-to-week for full 12 weeks. Squat, bench, deadlift, OHP rotate slots but don't get swapped. Accessories can rotate every 3-week phase. Variety in load/rep, not exercise.
- **Predictable session length 45–60 min**, coach refuses to extend.
- **Always-doable minimum (the "didn't break the streak" session):** 15 min, top set + 1 backoff on day's main lift, no accessories, just band/scap warm-up. Coach explicitly names it "minimum viable session." Logging counts as full streak credit.
- **Poacher rule alignment:** volume must be hard but completable. Coach should never threaten lockout for declining a 6th accessory — only for skipping the main lift. Compound is sacred; accessories negotiable down (never up).

**Time-of-day programming:**

- Morning (5:30–7am): CNS cold, joints stiff, shoulder/neck tightness peaks. Bias grindy/RPE-known/moderate-load. AVOID heavy singles, max-effort top sets, explosive variants (cleans, jumps, snatches) before 8am.
- Evening (6–8pm): CNS warmed. Bias autoregulated work, AMRAP, top-set RPE 9, novel exercises, conditioning finishers.
- **Programming rule:** Lift logged before 7am → coach auto-applies −5% load on prescribed top set, treats as floor not ceiling. Erik can hit prescribed if it feels good; not held to it.

**Adherence/streak triggers (concrete, not vague):**

- 5 consecutive sessions → unlock optional novel-exercise slot next session (3 sets, his pick from equipment list).
- 10 consecutive → unlock +1 accessory option for rest of phase.
- Missed 1 session → no coach reaction. Resume.
- Missed 2 consecutive → 3-day reset. First session back is minimum viable only. No accessories. No deficit reduction. Reset rhythm.
- Missed 3 consecutive → week-level reassessment. Drop to start of current 3-week phase, not where calendar says. Cut continues, lift restarts phase.
- Trail-Sat completed → Sunday rest mandatory. Coach refuses to log Sunday lift for 24 hr post-trail.
- Bodyweight target hit early → cut auto-switches to maintenance for remaining weeks.

**What other agents will likely get wrong:**

- Linear weekly +5 lb progression. Reality: plateaus week 4–6, selective regression week 8–9. Build wave loading within phases, not weekly +5.
- Tuesday/Thursday split assumption. UCSD academic calendar = conferences, late meetings, travel. Program should be **slot-based not day-locked**: "the squat session," "the bench session," "the trail run." Erik moves them within the week.
- Standard 2–3 min rest. With 45-min session cap, 3-min × 5 sets eats budget. Either rest shorter (90s accessories, 2 min main) or volume drops.
- Equipment optimism. Other agents see the inventory and prescribe leg-curl, microloading, cables. All need substitutes ready.
- Skipping shoulder warm-up cost. Mandatory 5–7 min eats 10% of budget.
- Trail-run fatigue carry-over. Sunday/Monday lower-body without flagging post-trail diff.
- Sycophancy in autoregulation. "Listen to your body, take a deload if you need it" = exactly the "your call" language banned. Triggers must be **numerical and prescribed** — RPE drift over X sessions = automatic deload.

**Open questions:**

1. What's actually on the rooftop? Specifically: leg curl machine, cable stack, lat pulldown, heaviest dumbbell. Photo or quick walk-through resolves ~30% of the program. **HIGHEST PRIORITY question.**
2. Trail-run Saturdays — confirmed every-other-week or weather/schedule dependent? If variable, need rule for "trail-run moved to Sunday this week" — consume Sunday rest, push Mon squat to Tue, or skip entirely?
3. Target weight and current weight? Determines whether week 1 is aggressive or week 10 is grinding. Drives volume/intensity tradeoff line.
4. Morning or evening default — your choice per-day, or one window structurally preferred? Decides which lift goes in which slot.
5. Refeed day placement — food-environment constraints? Friday family dinner = easier; Friday poor cooking night = harder. Programming choice depends on actual life logistics.

## Cross-Agent Convergence

All three agents agree on:

- **Hold intensity, cut volume** in deficit. Volume preservation is the wrong lever.
- **Wave loading > linear progression** in a 12-week cut.
- **Mandatory shoulder/neck warm-up** before any pressing or pulling.
- **Behind-neck press, upright row, heavy shrugs** — excluded outright.
- **Same exercises week-to-week within a phase** for adherence; vary load/reps not selection.
- **Session length cap 45–60 min**.
- **Minimum viable session** as a coded fallback for bad days, not skipping.
- **Trail-run Saturday is full lower-body equivalent.** Sunday rest is mandatory after.
- **No microloading**, jumps are 5 lb upper / 10 lb lower minimum.
- **Numerical autoregulation triggers**, not "listen to your body."

## Open Questions Common to ≥2 Agents

1. **True 1RM estimates / current bests** on the four main lifts. (Strength, Adaptation)
2. **Deficit magnitude** in kcal/day, week 1 to week 12. (Strength, Hypertrophy)
3. **Trail-run consistency** — fixed every-other-Sat, weather-dependent, or fluid? (Hypertrophy, Adaptation)
4. **Target weight + current weight + sleep data**. (Hypertrophy, Adaptation)
5. **Equipment ground truth** — what's actually on the PB rooftop? (Adaptation; the others assumed full inventory)
